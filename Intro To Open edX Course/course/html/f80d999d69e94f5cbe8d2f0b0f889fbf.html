This video, produced by the Marketing Working Group, shows some of the differences between the Open edX platform and Moodle, another e-learning technology platform. This video may be interesting if you are researching the Open edX platform in order to decide if it is right for you and your organization. The video elaborates on the key differences between the two platforms, so that you can choose the one that best suits your organization's needs.

The comparison between Open edX and Moodle will help you understand what makes our e-learning platform different and highlight the key features of our software capacity, structure, scalability, and audience.

Key points:

  • Moodle is geared towards small (15-30 students), in person, K-12 learning environments while the Open edX platform was created for scalable online courses (MOOCs, or massive open online courses)
  • Moodle is based in PHP, an older programming language. Open edX software is built in the more modern languages and frameworks of Python/Django and JavaScript/React.
  • Both are open-source, both feature interactive problem types, and both are highly customizable
  • Open edX software is highly accessible, meeting WCAG 2.2 compliance standards
  • Both have a plugin ecosystem, but the Open edX plugin ecosystem is broader and more robust
  • Unlike the Open edX platform, Moodle doesn't provide build in authentication/authorization
