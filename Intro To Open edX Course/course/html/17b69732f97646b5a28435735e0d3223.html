• 2012: edX  was founded as a joint venture between Harvard University and MIT.
  • 2012: Resounding success in of the first course on edx.org, which had over 155k students from over 162 countries.
  • 2013: The Open edX platform was released as open-source.
  • 2013: Shortly after its release as open-source, the first pull request from outside of the edX organization was done by Xavier Antoviaque.
  • 2014 - 2020 Open edX project is mainly edX-focused. The community using the project is growing but lacks involvement in product and development decisions.
  • 2020: the Core Contributor program was founded, bringing the Open edX community closer to the project by empowering them to take the lead on projects and decisions that affected the project's software. In 2020, this program began with 9 coders.
  • 2021: The Core Contributor program is expanded to have many roles that community members can fill, such as Translator, Product Manager, UI/UX designer, Marketing Specialist, Project Manager, and more!
  • 2021: The edX business was sold to 2U, and the proceeds were used to fund the continuing nonprofit now known as Axim Collaborative. Axim is focused on expanding and equalizing open source community participation in the Open edX project.

Since 2021, the community has become much more connected to decision making, with Core Contributors in areas such as coding, product management, UI/UX, and more diving into collaboration and co-creation of new and improved projects & features within the Open edX ecosystem.
