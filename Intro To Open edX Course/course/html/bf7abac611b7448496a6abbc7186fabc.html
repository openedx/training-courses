There are many types of contributions that the Open edX project needs. We strongly recommend attending a working group meeting to get up to speed with a group's work and jump into contributions in that area. Speaking with a working group has the advantage that your efforts will go in the right direction.

Finding Contribution Areas

Non-code contributions

There are many working groups looking for contributions. As of May 2023, here's a bit of information about the existing non-code related working groups:

    • The Product Working Group is on the lookout for product-minded people interested in triaging pull requests and providing product expertise in evaluating new feature proposals as well as leading new feature development. The Product Working Group is comprised of many subgroups covering areas such as LTI, on-campus learning, and the core product.
    • The Educators Working Group is an explicitly inviting space that is "no code" or "low code" friendly. We aim to be a community that seeks ways to support the community of Open edX learners, educators, and developers where participants can come for support and community, or share their insights and ideas. 
    • The Marketing Working Group desires people interested in promoting the Open edX platform via a number of marketing channels, such as YouTube videos, organizing meetups, and more
    • The Translations Working Group is looking for translators from all languages who can dedicate time to get their language translated to 100% - and sustain at that level

See our list of working groups, which updates and changes regularly, for more information about each group and how to join.
