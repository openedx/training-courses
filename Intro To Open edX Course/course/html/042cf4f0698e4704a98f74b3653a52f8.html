If you're a coder, you're probably interested in writing some code that adds new functionality or fixes some bugs. What's the best way to go about doing this?

Fixing Bugs

If you find bugs, we'd love for you to fix them! A common inclination is to do all the work of fixing the bug and then making a pull request. For very small bugs, this is an OK approach, but for larger bugs, we recommend you reach out to the repo's maintainer to make sure what you think is a bug is actually a bug, and for tips on the best way to tackle the bug. If the repo you're looking at doesn't have a maintainer or you can't figure out how to find them, make a post in the Development section of Discourse asking for advice.

Developing New Features

We're so excited to hear about all the neat new ideas everyone in the community has. However, as a large project worked on by people who are mostly full time employees of companies that have their own priorities, it is difficult to accept a new implemented feature when it lands on our doorstep.
