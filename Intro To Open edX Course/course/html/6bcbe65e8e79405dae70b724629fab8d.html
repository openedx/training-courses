Note: This section dives into the Product Offering, discussed earlier. You don't need to understand this information to participate in the Open edX project, but we are providing it in case it is of interest to you.

The following diagram is a simplified version of what the Product Offering contains. It is broken up into various tiers (the colored boxes) and there is a distinction between the Core Product (what we just discussed) and the Full Catalog, which is the huge set of extra extensions you can use on top of a basic installation.

Along with Official Extensions, we also have the notion of Unofficial Extensions. These extensions are not a part of the Open edX project, but they are frequently developed by members of the Open edX community and do some cool things. The difference between Official and Unofficial extensions is simply the level of support the main project provides for them.

Core Product: The community will support the Core Product as its top priority. [As a technical matter the Core Product contains three distinct sets of features: the "baked in" features (things like checkbox problems), "extension frameworks" (behind the scenes code that lets you use extensions like XBlocks and LTI), and "Bundled Extensions" (things like Open Response Assessment - these extensions we feel are useful to almost all users of the Open edX software).]

Official Extensions: The community will try to support Official Extensions, and it will do so largely by delegating responsibility [...] If the community cannot consistently support or maintain an Official Extension, then it may be reclassified as Unofficial.

Unofficial Extensions: The Open edX community will not directly support Unofficial Extensions, and they will not be the focus of the Open edX project or its working groups. Of course, this does not preclude individual community members or teams from working on or supporting these components themselves. If the community demonstrates both consistent demand and consistent support for an Unofficial Extension, then it may be reclassified as Official.

If you want to learn more about this topic, take a look at the OEP-57.
