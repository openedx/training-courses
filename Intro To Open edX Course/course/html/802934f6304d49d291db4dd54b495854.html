Open edX Proposals are foundational governance and architectural documents for the Open edX Community and codebase. The majority of Open edX Proposals - or, "OEPs" - pertain to the codebase: how major features should be architected, primarily. Some OEPs cover the processes by which our community operates: we have ones for Core Contributors, one for Maintainers, and one for the Core Product. Others are more technical in nature, meant for those who are coding Open edX software.

In more detail: an OEP is a document that details a specific technology or community decision being made by the Open edX community, in the form of a best practice, architecture design, or process adjustment. An OEP should provide the use cases and rationales that surround that choice. OEPs are not the only way for a change to be made to Open edX, however, the goal is to create a collection of OEP documents as a repository or knowledge archive of large and broadly relevant choices made for the platform.

You are welcome to read any and all published OEPs on the open-edx-proposals.readthedocs.io site.
