Description

A product roadmap is a high-level, strategic document that maps out the general stages of a product’s development. Specifically, the main purpose of the Open edX Roadmap, as Eden Huthmacher mentions, is:

  • To comprehensively document all current, planned work in-flight, across the Open edX ecosystem
  • To provide insight into early-stage projects that may not be planned but are in the pipeline and may impact the Open edX ecosystem in significant ways.
  • To provide a framework for documenting and prioritizing ideas for future development and investment.

What is our priority?

The Open edX Roadmap can be found here. As you can see, the project board has several columns. It also has tabs listed across the top. Let's take a quick dive into the structure and organization of the board.

As you can see in the previous images, the Roadmap is a kanban-style board, organized into eight main status categories:

  • Ideation: A collection of early-stage ideas for possible contributions to the Open edX platform. Ideation tickets consist of simple opportunity briefs outlining the reason behind the idea and the basic value-add.
  • Prioritized: Subset of ideation tickets that have been identified by the Product Working Group for further discovery.
  • Backlog: Initiatives that are intended for contribution, are in the backlog at any organization in the community.
  • In-progress: Initiatives that are intended for contribution that are in-flight. In-progress tickets must contain information about: the initiative goal; context; scope and approach; value and impact.
  • Done: To Ship in a Future Named Release: Work on an initiative is complete and it may be live for Marketplace ProvidersOpens a link in a new tab, but the Initiative has not yet been shipped in an Open edX Named Release.
  • To Ship in [Named Release] or Shipped in [Named Release]: Work on an Initiative is included in a specific Named Release.
  • Feature Tickets - Product Pull Requests: Community Contributions that are currently being reviewed by Open edX Product Managers.

The tabs across the top of the Board are organized by main platform area, and aggregate all of the tickets related to major platform areas and key large features. For example, if you want to see all of the tickets associated with the Discussion Forums, click on the tab for “Discussion Forums”. 

It is important to highlight that roadmap views are a current work-in-progress and are expected to change over time.
