A brief break for some common questions, and their answers!

In the last section (Join Us - Communication Tools), we covered the many ways of communicating with the Open edX community: Discourse, Slack, Confluence, docs.openedx.org, GitHub... whew! It's a lot. So how should you get in touch with us, when you need help? Here's a Q&A.

Q: I am trying to run an Open edX instance, and I'm running into trouble. How do I get help?

A: First, check out the Site Operators section of the docs website. Didn't find what you're looking for? Try browsing the Site Operators category in Discourse, and if no one's asked your question before, open a new question!

Q: I want to jump into writing code for the Open edX platform. Where do I start?

A: There's an introduction to contributing code in Module 3 of this course that covers how to get started.

In general, we'd recommend starting with the Developer Onboarding Course and the Developers section of the docs site. If you have questions, you should check out the Developers section of Discourse - many common questions have already been answered there. Of course, ask your questions there if you can't find answers elsewhere!

Q: I'm interested in observing a working group meeting.

A: Find information about working groups at openedx.org/wgs - there you'll find a list of all active working groups, and a Google calendar that shows their meeting times. You're free to join any meeting at any time - the link to join the video call will be on each invitation.

Q: I want to participate in a working group / help the working group do work!

A: Attending meetings as described above is the first, and biggest, step to joining a working group. You may also want to join Slack and then join the working group chat room (all working group chat names start with "wg-").

Q: I'm a course author/educator. Are there any community resources available to me about running a course?

A: Yes!! There is a growing amount of material on the Educators section of docs.openedx.org, and there is a helpful guide about running a course on edx.readthedocs.io. There is also an Educators section on Discourse, where many people get together and ask the very tough questions about how to teach, and how to do it well. 

As of April 2023, an Educators Working Group has been created. You can find more detail on the Open edX wiki.
