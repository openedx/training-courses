The Ideal State

As you are working on any given Open edX repo, you will actually have a local repo with two remotes:

      1. “origin” will point to your personal fork in your account, and
      2. “upstream” will point to the repo in the openedx GitHub org.

Very briefly, the normal flow for writing code contributed upstream will be:

    • Pull from the upstream into your fork to get the latest code.
    • Make a feature branch in your repo.
    • Make your changes, and commit them.
    • Push your branch to your fork (origin).
    • Make a pull request against upstream (more on this process in Module 5)

The repository maintainers will then take a look at your pull request, providing review and helping you navigate the process.
