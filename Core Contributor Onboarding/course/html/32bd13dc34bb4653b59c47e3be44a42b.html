You may already be a part of one or more Working Groups, but if you're not we encourage you to join one! Note that hours spent in Working Group meetings count as Core Contributor hours, as do hours spent on any follow-up work.

We have many working groups, and are always adding and removing groups as fits community need. Here's a sample of a few groups:

  • The Marketing working group focuses on marketing the Open edX project and driving connections between community members. They've been successful with putting on a monthly Open edX meetup with interesting and varied speakers. A subcommittee of this group also focuses on putting on the yearly Open edX conference.
  • The Build-Test-Release working group does two months of heavy lifting twice per year as they prep, test, and release a new Open edX Named Release. Expertise in any of the following areas would be more than welcome: coding/developing; written English for documentation; knowledge of platform features (to perform testing); project management (no technical experience required).
  • The Frontend working group is a collection of developers focused on moving forward the state of our frontend ecosystem. Particular focus is on standardization and improvement of MFEs, as well as which new ones to build and how to get them adopted. Developers interested in frontend architecture, MFEs, and/or Paragon will find this group interesting.

For more on Working Groups, including Slack rooms and meeting times, please see the Working Group wiki page.
